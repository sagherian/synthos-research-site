In plain English

Accenture is the world's largest consulting and technology-services firm — the people companies and governments hire to install new software, run their IT, and now to roll out AI. Think of it as a giant "outsourced brain and hands" for big organizations, with 801,000 employees.

The stock has been cut roughly in half in the past year. Two things are true at once: (1) the business is genuinely good — it still earns a 25% return on equity, has more cash than debt, and throws off huge free cash flow; and (2) investors are scared that AI could do a lot of what Accenture's people get paid to do, so they now pay only about 11 dollars for every dollar of annual profit (cheap for a company this quality). That fear is the whole story.

Our verdict is Watch: it looks cheap and it's well-run, but the stock is still falling and the AI question is unresolved. We'd rather see growth stop shrinking before buying.

Here's what our three scores mean in everyday terms:

  • Downside Risk 4/10 (below average risk). It's financially very safe and already cheap — but a stock dropping this hard can keep dropping, and the AI threat is real.
  • Growth Quality 6/10 (good, not great). A high-quality, profitable business, but its sales growth has slowed to low-single-digits.
  • Exponential Potential 3/10 (low). Big, slow-growing, and slowing further. This is a value/income idea, not a rocket ship.

The one big worry: if AI lets clients do the work with far fewer consultants, Accenture's growth engine — billing lots of people's hours — could shrink for years.

1. What it is

Accenture plc (NYSE: ACN) is a global professional-services firm — the largest of its kind — providing strategy, consulting, technology, and managed services (formerly "outsourcing") to enterprises and governments worldwide. Founded 1951, headquartered in Dublin, Ireland; CEO Julie Sweet; ~801,000 employees. Fiscal year ends August 31.

The business splits cleanly into two engines and five industry verticals. Its offerings today center on cloud migration, application modernization, data/AI platforms, cybersecurity, and — increasingly — enterprise generative-AI transformation, which is simultaneously its biggest new bookings driver and the source of the market's fear.

Revenue mix (FY2025, from filings — $69.7B total):

  • By type of work: Consulting $35.1B (50%) · Managed Services (Outsourcing) $34.6B (50%) — a deliberately balanced book; managed services is the stickier, more recurring half.
  • By industry (FY24 basis): Products $19.6B · Health & Public Service $13.8B · Financial Services $11.6B · Communications, Media & Technology $10.8B · Resources $9.1B — genuinely diversified, no single industry >30%.
  • By geography (Q3 FY26 run-rate): Americas ~$9.1B/qtr (~49%) · EMEA ~$6.9B (~37%) · Asia Pacific ~$2.7B (~14%). US federal is a specific soft spot — management guides an estimated ~1% full-year revenue drag from it (§9).

No single-customer concentration risk; the concentration risk here is thematic (AI vs. labor arbitrage), not client-level.

4. Exponential Potential

Synthos separates compounders (durable high returns on capital) from exponentials (accelerating, multi-baggers-from-here). ACN is a high-quality compounder that is currently decelerating, not accelerating — the opposite of the exponential profile:

  • Forward growth: revenue CAGR FY25→FY30E ~7.0% ($69.7B → $97.6B, est.); EPS CAGR ~9.0% ($12.29 → $18.92 est.). Solid, not spectacular.
  • Acceleration (the 2nd derivative) is negative: revenue growth was +7.4% (FY25) and estimates step down to +5.7% (FY26E), +4.0% (FY27E), then re-firm to +5.2% (FY28E). The near-term trend is deceleration, and organic growth in the latest print was only +3% in local currency — the whole bear case.
  • Room to run: the addressable market (global IT services + the new enterprise-AI transformation wave) is large, but Accenture is already the scale leader at $70B revenue. There is no small-cap "room to run" tailwind; a 3× from here implies a ~$250B company competing against its own labor model.
  • The AI wild card cuts both ways: the same generative-AI wave that could disintermediate billable hours is also the largest new-demand catalyst management cites ("more large-scale AI transformation programs," §9). Exponential potential would require AI to land firmly as a tailwind — currently unproven.

Exponential Potential: Low (3/10). Own ACN, if at all, for cheap durable cash generation and a growing dividend, not for a fast multibagger. A small, accelerating name with these returns on capital would score far higher; a decelerating $84B leader does not.

5. Financials (real numbers — FMP annual/quarterly)

  • Revenue: FY25 $69.67B, +7.4% (FY24 $64.90B +1.2%; FY23 $64.11B). Q3 FY26 (latest) $18.72B, +6% USD / +3% local currency YoY — steady but slow.
  • Margins: gross ~32% TTM, operating ~17% (Q3 FY26 operating margin 17.0%, +20 bps YoY), net ~10.7% TTM. Margins are stable-to-slightly-expanding — the deceleration is a top-line story, not a profitability one.
  • Returns on capital (the quality tell): ROE 25.0%, ROIC 16.9%, ROCE 23.0% — genuinely high-quality economics for a people business.
  • Earnings: FY25 net income $7.69B, EPS $12.29 (diluted $12.15). TTM EPS ~$12.78. Q3 FY26 diluted EPS $3.80, +9% YoY — EPS growing faster than revenue on buybacks and mix.
  • Cash flow: FY25 operating CF $11.47B, capex only −$0.6B, FCF $10.87B (~16% of revenue; ~13% FCF yield on market cap). Capital-light and cash-rich — a structural strength.
  • Balance sheet: net cash — total debt $8.2B vs. cash/investments $11.5B → net debt −$3.3B, net-debt/EBITDA −0.1×. Fortress. Goodwill/intangibles ~$25B (40% of assets) from serial acquisitions is the one item to watch for impairment if growth stalls.

6. Valuation — priced in or room?

This is the crux: ACN is cheap on every trailing and forward metric it has been expensive on for a decade.

  • ~11× trailing EPS (vs. a historical 25–30× range), ~10× FY26E, ~9× FY27E.
  • EV/EBITDA 6.7×, EV/Sales 1.1×, P/FCF ~6.7×, FCF yield ~13%, dividend yield 4.6% (payout ~50%, easily covered).
  • FMP letter rating "A" (overall score 4/5; ROE and ROA score 5/5; DCF score 5/5).

The question is not "is it cheap" (it is) but "is it a value trap." The multiple compressed because the market is pricing a structural growth problem, not a cyclical dip. Our base case gives back only a modest re-rate (to ~11.6×) on FY27 EPS — we do not underwrite a return to 20×+ because we can't yet prove AI is a tailwind. Street targets (context): consensus $198, high $282, low $130 — a huge spread that says the Street itself hasn't resolved the AI debate. Our $170 base is deliberately more conservative than consensus. Cheap and high-quality — but only a bargain if the growth stops eroding.

8. Moat & competitive position

Accenture's moat is real but is exactly what's being tested: (1) scale and breadth — the largest, most diversified services firm, able to staff global, multi-year transformations no boutique can; (2) deep enterprise relationships — 104 bookings of $100M+ year-to-date, +13%, i.e. it sits inside the C-suite of most of the Fortune Global 500; (3) a balanced consulting/managed-services book where the managed-services half is sticky and recurring. The threat is that generative AI compresses the billable-hours model that underlies consulting economics — and Accenture must cannibalize its own labor arbitrage to stay ahead.

Peer set (FMP-listed, market cap): the FMP peer list is a loose "IT / tech" bucket rather than pure services comps — Adobe $87B, Applied Materials $479B, Amphenol $202B, Infosys $45B (the closest true services comp), KLA $308B, Leidos $14B, Palo Alto Networks $237B, Qualcomm $186B, Sony $122B, Texas Instruments $267B. Against its genuine peers (Infosys, TCS, Cognizant, IBM Consulting, Capgemini), Accenture is the scale and margin leader — but the entire Indian-heritage services group faces the same AI question, and several trade at similar de-rated multiples.

9. Management, capital allocation & guidance

  • Capital allocation: disciplined and shareholder-friendly. FY25 returned cash via a $4.6B buyback and $3.7B dividend; the dividend was just raised 10% to $1.63/quarter. ~$3.2B repurchase authority remaining. Capex is trivial (~1% of revenue), so nearly all FCF is returnable. Serial tuck-in M&A ($1.5B in FY25; the Dragos/runZero/NetRise OT-security deals announced in Q3) extends capability — the goodwill build is the offsetting watch-item.
  • Insider activity: the only recent Form 4s (2026-06-08) are routine equity awards to officers (CEO Sweet, CFO Park, COO Hogan, etc.) at $179.76 — grants, not open-market buys or sells. No signal either way.
  • Management's own guidance — FY26, from the SEC 8-K earnings release dated 2026-06-18 (management's self-interested words; half-weighted by house policy):

- Full-year revenue growth 3–4% local currency (4–5% ex an estimated ~1% US-federal drag) — a downgrade from the prior 3–5% range, and the tangible sign of the deceleration.

- GAAP diluted EPS $13.38–$13.50 (+10–11% over FY25); adjusted EPS $13.78–$13.90 (+7–8%).

- GAAP operating margin ~15.3% (~60 bps expansion); free cash flow $10.8–$11.5B; capital return at least $9.5B.

- CEO framing: "demand for large-scale reinvention remains strong… more large-scale AI transformation programs." Management is positioning AI as the demand driver, not the threat — read with the appropriate half-weight, since they are talking their own book. The trimmed revenue range is the more objective tell.

10. Catalysts & what to watch

  • Next earnings: 2026-09-24 (Q4 FY26; Street EPS $3.21, revenue ~$18.05B). The report that sets full-year FY27 guidance — the single most important event for the thesis.
  • New bookings & book-to-bill: Q3 bookings were $19.3B, −2% USD / −3% local currency YoY. Bookings re-accelerating (or not) is the leading indicator of whether growth has bottomed.
  • GenAI bookings disclosure: management quantifies AI-related bookings each quarter — the clearest read on whether AI is net tailwind or net threat.
  • Organic (local-currency) revenue growth: watch for the ~3% LC number to firm or fade; FX is currently a ~+2% flatterer that could reverse.
  • US federal: the ~1% drag stabilizing or worsening.

Thesis tripwires (what would change the call):

  • Upgrade to Buy if: organic (LC) growth re-accelerates above ~5% and the stock reclaims and holds its 50-DMA — i.e. both fundamental and technical bottoms confirm.
  • Downgrade to Avoid if: two consecutive quarters of declining local-currency revenue, or a goodwill impairment — evidence the AI disintermediation is real and structural.

11. Key risks

  • AI disintermediation (structural, the whole story): generative AI could compress the billable-hours consulting model faster than Accenture re-platforms onto it. This is the reason for the −55% de-rate and the reason we say Watch.
  • Value trap: cheap can get cheaper if growth keeps eroding; a 9–11× multiple can persist for years if the market decides the terminal growth rate is near zero.
  • Growth deceleration: revenue growth already trimmed to 3–4% LC for FY26; momentum is the wrong way.
  • US federal / government exposure: an explicit ~1% revenue drag guided by management.
  • Goodwill/intangible impairment: ~$25B (40% of assets) from serial M&A is at risk of write-down if growth stalls.
  • FX: a current ~+2% tailwind that flatters headline USD growth and could reverse.
  • No expert corroboration: zero Synthos KB coverage — we have no independent panel to pressure-test the quant read, which is itself a reason for caution.

12. Verdict, position sizing & monitoring

Watch. Accenture is a genuinely high-quality, net-cash, cash-generative franchise (25% ROE, ~13% FCF yield, 4.6% growing dividend) trading at a decade-low ~11× earnings — the fundamentals and valuation lean bullish. But the verdict is held at Watch, not Buy, for three honest reasons: (1) a −55% primary downtrend with no confirmed bottom (a falling knife); (2) a live, unresolved secular question — whether AI is friend or foe to the core model — that we cannot yet answer in ACN's favor; and (3) zero expert coverage in our KB, so there is no conviction panel to corroborate the quant case. Cheap and good is not the same as safe and timely.

  • Sizing: watch-list. If initiating, a ~1–2% starter only after a base forms (higher low + 50-DMA reclaim, or a bookings re-acceleration on the 2026-09-24 print). Do not average into a knife.
  • Monitoring: re-underwrite on the §10 tripwires; formal re-score at Q4 FY26 earnings when FY27 guidance lands. This verdict is logged as a tracked Synthos call as of 2026-07-03 at $137.35.
  • Single biggest risk: AI disintermediating the labor-based consulting model faster than Accenture can pivot onto it — the question the whole stock now hinges on.
