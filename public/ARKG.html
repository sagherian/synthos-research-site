What this fund is

ARKG is actively managed — there is no index. ARK Investment Management (Cathie Wood) picks ~30–40 high-conviction companies it believes will benefit from the genomics revolution: CRISPR gene editing, DNA sequencing, molecular diagnostics, and AI-driven drug discovery.

It is highly concentrated: around 33 holdings, with the top 10 near 60% of the fund and individual positions running 4–8%. Those are far larger single-name bets than any passive fund here. Sector is ~98% health care, skewed to unprofitable small/mid-cap disruptors.

It is a fully transparent active ETF — ARK publishes holdings and even daily trades. Return dispersion is driven by a handful of large positions and by the manager's discretion, not by a rule.

Honest fit

The job it does

  • Concentrated, high-conviction access to the genomics/gene-editing frontier in one line.
  • Exposure to unprofitable early-stage disruptors most broad-biotech funds underweight.
  • A fully transparent active vehicle — you can see exactly what ARK owns and trades.

What it does not do

  • Is not diversified — top-10 ~60%, so a few positions drive everything; far more volatile than XBI.
  • Is not a broad biotech/pharma proxy — it skews to unprofitable small/mid-cap genomics, not large-cap drugs.
  • Is not passive or cheap — 0.75% fee plus manager risk; performance hinges on ARK's stock-picking.

What would change this read (falsifiers)

  • A rate up-cycle punishes long-duration, cash-burning growth — ARKG's exact profile — most severely.
  • Manager / process risk: ARK's discretionary calls can be wrong for years; the fund has a history of extreme drawdowns.
  • If you want the genomics theme without single-manager and concentration risk, a broad biotech fund is the lower-variance route.
