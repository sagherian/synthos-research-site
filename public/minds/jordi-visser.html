1 · How the world works, according to Visser

His five strongest invariants — beliefs restated across years of claims without a single reversal in the record — followed by the hub his current worldview hangs on.

Technology and AI are structurally deflationary

Held March 2021 → 2026 · never reversed · his oldest belief

Intelligence getting cheaper collapses the price of everything built from it. This was a background belief for years — until late 2025, when it moved to the center of everything else he thinks (see section 4).

Technology is structurally deflationary — March 2021
AI makes intelligence free and abundant, driving structural deflation — February 2026

Bitcoin is the core scarce store-of-value holding

Held March 2021 → April 2026 · never reversed at thesis level

The conviction survived two of his own dead derivations (the liquidity story and the halving cycle — he killed both, see section 4), two publicly retracted price targets, and a 60%+ drawdown. The thesis stayed; the reasons under it were rebuilt.

Bitcoin is an emerging store of value — March 2021
Bitcoin is the one AI cannot dilute or disrupt — April 2026

Debasement, not CPI, is the real story

Held May 2023 → February 2026

Asset prices rise mostly because the currency they are measured in loses value. Inflation prints are noise; the denominator is the signal.

The real story is debasement, not CPI — October 2023
governments will print to infinity to fight it, forcing money toward hard assets — February 2026

Central banks are prisoners of markets

Held August 2020 → April 2026

Every deleveraging ends the same way: governments are forced to intervene. In his model this is not a policy choice, it is a structural inevitability — and it is why he treats crisis lows as buyable.

as markets rely on it, it becomes their prisoner — August 2020
Every deleveraging shock (2020, 2022, 2023, 2025) required government intervention to bottom — 2026

The system requires ever-more debt, so rates cannot stay above growth

Held August 2020 → April 2026

Monetization and financial repression are, to him, arithmetic rather than forecast. The predicted rate levels changed repeatedly over the years; the mechanism never did.

the entire system requires ever-more debt — 2023
the Fed cannot Volcker-fight inflation like 2022 — 2026

The hub of the current era: abundant intelligence

In place since December 2, 2025 (see section 4 for the pivot)

Since late 2025, everything above routes through one idea: AI makes intelligence abundant, abundance destroys business moats, and capital responds by hunting scarcity — hard assets, energy, and Bitcoin. His Bitcoin case is now derived from AI, and explicitly not from liquidity, not from halving cycles, and not from inflation hedging.

As AI shrinks every company's lifespan, Bitcoin becomes the only borderless digital-economy asset — December 2025
In deflation, capital seeks scarcity — 2026

4 · What he recently changed his mind about

We log mind-changes as a feature, not an embarrassment. A voice that never updates is not thinking; a record that hides updates is not honest. The beliefs below carry death dates because he killed them himself, on the record.

The pivot: December 2, 2025 — he replaced the hub of his worldview

For five years his master variable was liquidity: central-bank balance sheets and fiat debasement drove all asset prices, and Bitcoin rode that cycle. Between September 13 and December 18, 2025, he dismantled that hub in public and replaced it with the AI-abundance worldview in section 1 — same Bitcoin conviction, rebuilt on entirely different reasons.

Beliefs he killed, with dates

  • "Liquidity drives Bitcoin" — first killed November 13, 2025. Bitcoin is driven by AI and the stock market, not liquidity. Re-confirmed dead December 18, 2025 (Contrary to the liquidity/QE bull case) and March 3, 2026 (Rejects the liquidity-drives-Bitcoin thesis as lazy).
  • The four-year halving cycle — killed February 18, 2026. There has never been a real four-year cycle. He had already demoted it in July 2023: the halving narrative is coincidental timing, not the driver.
  • Bitcoin as an inflation hedge — inverted March 3, 2026. The mechanism now runs the other way: Bitcoin works best during deflation.

His stated trigger: AI operating leverage showing up in earnings — AI-driven headcount reduction plus natural attrition is the 'new QE' (September 13, 2025) — and, by his own later dating, a capability jump: Opus 4.5 (late November) sharply accelerated AI (April 2026).

Honest detail: the pivot was a window, not a day. Ten days after first rejecting the liquidity mechanism he was still asserting the old wiring — rate cuts, accelerating earnings, and liquidity all swimming in the same direction (November 23, 2025). Real minds change messily; the record keeps both.

5 · Where he is silent

A model that can say "no belief here" is worth more than one that always answers. Two kinds of silence — and they mean different things.

Actively rejected — he has said no, on the record

  • Liquidity drives Bitcoin. Rejects the liquidity-drives-Bitcoin thesis as lazy — March 2026.
  • Bitcoin as inflation hedge. Inverted: Bitcoin works best during deflation — March 2026.
  • The four-year cycle. There has never been a real four-year cycle — February 2026.
  • AI as a bubble about to burst. a government-backed necessity, not a bubble; corrections are rotations, and his bear cases exist only as the gated conditionals in section 3.
  • Geopolitics as a trade. Crowd catastrophes never work as trades; on Taiwan: disrupting it would halt AGI/ASI, so kinetic war over it is self-defeating.
  • Quantum computing breaking Bitcoin. Quantum is not a threat to Bitcoin for at least five years.

Never asserted — six years of claims, zero mechanism

  • Tariffs → inflation or markets. No tariff mechanism anywhere in 2020–2026 — a striking absence for a macro commentator in 2025–26.
  • AI job losses → demand collapse → recession. Labor displacement is always a corporate-margin story in his model, never demand destruction.
  • Wages → sticky inflation. The Phillips-curve channel never appears.
  • Deficits → bond vigilantes. He asserts monetization and repression instead; deficits never appear as a rates shock.
  • China stimulus → global reflation. Absent even through his 2026 commodity-bull turn.
  • Housing wealth → consumer spending. Real estate appears only as rate-sensitive leverage or a tokenizable asset.
