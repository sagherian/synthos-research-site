The Delta — what changed in expert conviction this week

The most important thing in markets is the rate of change. This page tracks how the conviction of the experts we follow moved, topic by topic, versus a recent baseline.

A synthesis of what the experts said this week — not a Synthos market call. For each of 33 tracked topics we measure a conviction-weighted net stance, then compare this week’s reading to the trailing 8-week baseline. The gap is the delta. “Conviction” is our model’s extracted read of how strongly a view was held — a signal, not the speaker’s own score. No grades, no scorekeeping.

This week’s conversation: 751 attributed claims — 335 bullish · 211 neutral · 205 bearish. 8 of 33 topics moved past the change threshold this week; 8 held, and 17 had too little signal to call.

How to read this — and its limits. This week’s reading reflects the voices who published this week measured against a broader recent baseline, so a delta can reflect either a genuine change of view or a change in who spoke. Read it as “the conversation this week vs. the recent baseline,” not as any single person reversing course. Two large, fully-undated sources (Jim Bianco and Anthony Pompliano) carry no dates and so are absent from the baseline — baseline coverage is understated and disclosed here rather than hidden.

Baseline window: dated claims from 2026-05-15 to 2026-07-06 (4,874 claims). Current window: this week’s batch of 751 claims. Conviction figures are model-extracted signals, not the speakers’ own numbers. Every claim traces to a real source in the Synthos knowledge base.
